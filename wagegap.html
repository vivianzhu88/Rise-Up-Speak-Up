Did You Know?

The raw wage gap data shows that over her lifetime, a woman would earn roughly 73.7% to 77% of what a man would. Data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics indicates the median earnings of female workers working full-time to be roughly 77% of the median earnings of their male counterparts.

Women are more likely to work over 40 hours a week, compared to 35-39 hours for men, in order to make up the wage gap.

The trend of men earning more than women is especially prevalent in traditionally male-dominated fields, specifically in low-skill jobs, where men make 6% more than women hourly.

Your Rights

The Equal Pay Act of 1963 mandates that wages cannot be disparaged due to the gender of the worker. However, a law and its enforcement are two different things. A law is nothing unless it's enforced.

Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 prohibits discrimination against employees based on things like gender, race, color, and sex. However, what is considered discrimination is up to the discretion of the company, with regards to company policy.
